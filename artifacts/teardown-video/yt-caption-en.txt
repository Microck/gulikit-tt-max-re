This time, we disassembled and tested the GuliKit TT Max
and compared it with the TT Pro.
The TT Max model is NS69 , and
the TT Pro model is NS68.
They each have two color options,
but they are almost identical in appearance.
I have to mention a few features: its
joysticks are TMR joysticks. The
joystick caps have anti-slip stripes,
and the joystick limits are inside, so
the joystick caps won't rub against the outer frame,
resulting in a smoother feel.
Within a 3% range,
it can achieve relatively stable and precise movements.
There is a force adjustment nut at the bottom of the joystick cap, which
allows for stepless adjustment of
the force within 720 degrees
, or two turns.
If you can't tell the direction of the force adjustment,
you can look at the direction markings on the nut.
Its vibration motor is not the common rotor motor; it is
a reciprocating vibration motor
developed and produced by GuliKit,
which can change the force
and vibration mode.

I tested the polling rate and resolution.
The controller's built-in Flash Connect 2 Bluetooth and direct Bluetooth connection to the PC
are much faster than the 100-200 Hz of ordinary Bluetooth. In actual
tests, it was above 650 Hz.
For TT's Bluetooth
, which is the "Flash Connect 2" receiver...
The official
refresh rate is 730Hz, but actual testing shows it exceeds that.
This FlashConnect 2 Bluetooth adapter
, like the Bluetooth module in the gamepad,
incorporates FlashConnect 2 Bluetooth technology.
This adapter
provides an independent channel for data transmission
, increasing connection speed and stability.
The FlashConnect 2 Bluetooth adapter also supports
compatibility with other popular gamepads on multiple platforms,
so it's not just a dedicated receiver for the TT gamepad.
For USB wired connections, the official refresh rate is 1000Hz, but actual
testing shows around 930Hz.
This isn't a gamepad issue
, but rather because my monitor was using a USB-C port,
making the USB bus extremely busy.
If you absolutely need 1000Hz,
I recommend connecting your monitor to a DisplayPort or HDMI port.

If your TT Max or TT Pro runs out of power,
the controller's vibration motor will be disabled
, and the GuliKit logo on the front will flash to indicate that
it is connected to the charger.
It will then activate 5V 0.5A charging.
Here are the charging statistics:
Initially, I didn't turn it off;
it was kept on while charging.
The charging power was 2.9W.
After 17 minutes, it automatically shut down,
and the power dropped to 2.5W.
The 2.5W power remained until
1 hour and 26 minutes
, then gradually decreased until fully
charged. It took 2 hours and 5 minutes to fully charge,
and the energy input was 4.799 Wh
, or 4.8 Wh.
The controller's battery is 3.7V 950mAh,
equivalent to 3.515 Wh.
Assuming an 80% conversion efficiency, the
actual energy input was 3.839 Wh,
which exceeds the rated energy value.
This means the battery capacity is not falsely advertised.
